"When you are authentic, your eyes glow, and with those eyes you can do anything." — A. N. Nesterenko, one of the creative leaders interviewed for my thesis

Hi, I'm Maru. I'm not looking for a job — I'm looking for the kind of work that makes my eyes glow.

A lot of people say that in their twenties. For me it isn't a feeling I'm chasing blind — it's a thesis. I spent over a year researching exactly this: what separates work that glows from work that doesn't. Eight interviews later, with people across Sila Sveta, Bureau Betak, Moment Factory and beyond, I think I finally understand the difference. And I know which side of it I want to be on.

It's hard to put into words what happens to me when I watch Iris van Herpen turn movement into fabric, or how Boris Acket makes me feel with a single beam of light. I get lost reading about how Betak built an empire out of swinging hammocks (iykyk), how Sila Sveta started with one slide projector, or how Es Devlin's path started in UK theatres before scaling into memory architecture. This is the art that fuels my obsession — and the feeling I want to replicate in my own work.

I know I'm capable of it — not because I already have every tool (I don't), but because I have persistence. For the past year, alongside university, I worked 40+ hours a week as the right hand — and only employee — to two independent fashion designers, building their operations, automation and e-commerce systems from scratch while teaching myself code, Adobe, DaVinci Resolve and AI tools after hours. The same proximity put me in photoshoots as a stylist and on production teams for events with the Vienna State Opera and art galleries — closer to stage and experience design than any job title suggests. The specifics live in my portfolio.

I've been self-taught since 17, when I started corporate internships before finishing high school. At 18, I packed my life into a suitcase and moved to Vienna alone. A day after I arrived I was coaching choreography to a cast with years of experience on me. Not long after, I landed inside an interior design studio — mentored, but trusted early with real client work on luxury properties — while teaching myself the tools I'd need for what came next. Four years later, the pattern hasn't changed: put me near people who inspire me — and if you're reading this, that's you — and I make myself useful, fast. That's not a promise. It's just what happens.

Degree: finished. Authentic creativity: researched. Competence: accumulated. Next stop — a creative technologist role, full time: somewhere that needs a creative who can build and think in systems.

Bilingual EN/RU · Based in Europe · Fully mobile for relocation
